# EXP-007: is the delta advantage robust to training budget?

- Hypothesis served: H2 robustness (the one exploitable weakness in the headline:
  all V2-vs-B2 comparisons sit at a single 4000-step budget)
- Date: pre-registered 2026-07-05, before any results
- Status: CONFIGURED

## Threat model

If delta layers merely OPTIMIZE faster, B2 catches up with more training and
"better architecture" collapses into "faster early training" — the H1 confound
class, inverted and pointed at us. Cheapest kill: double the budget.

## Design

V2-delta vs B2-6L on algo_exec, 8000 steps (2x standard), lr 4.9e-4, seeds 0-1
(pre-registered at 2 seeds: algo_exec has the suite's tightest seed variance —
V2 .970/.970/.955, gains +.235/+.205/+.215 — and this is a trend read, not a
margin gate). Reference gap at 4000 steps: +21.8 (EXP-002-AX, 3 seeds).

## Pre-registered read (in order)

1. **Ceiling guard**: if V2@8000 all-tier mean > 0.95, the primary read moves to
   difficulty tiers 3-5 (ceiling compression would fake a shrinking gap).
2. **Budget-robust (PASS)**: gap(8000) >= 0.5 x gap(4000) on the primary read
   (i.e. >= +10.9 all-tier, or the tier-3-5 equivalent) -> claim upgraded to
   "budget-robust"; 50M scale check proceeds.
3. **Confound confirmed**: gap(8000) < 0.5 x gap(4000) -> honest reframe
   ("delta accelerates optimization at small budgets"), 50M spend cancelled
   (it would inherit the confound), report reframed accordingly.
4. Secondary: record whether B2@8000 exceeds B2@4000 at all (if B2 is
   data/step-saturated, the 2x arm can't discriminate and verdict is
   INCONCLUSIVE-EXTEND, one more doubling on B2 only).

## Cost

4 jobs, one session shared with EXP-005-DEN (~$4.5 total).

## Results

4/4 jobs clean (session 3 pod, 2026-07-05). algo_exec, 8000 steps, seeds 0-1:

| model | all-tier | tier 3-5 mean |
|---|---|---|
| V2-delta @8000 | .985 / .990 | .979 |
| B2-6L @8000 | .785 / .805 | .721 |

- **Ceiling guard TRIGGERED** (V2 > .95 all-tier) -> primary read is tiers 3-5.
- **Primary read (tier 3-5): gap@8000 = +25.8** vs the pass bar of half the
  4000-step gap. All-tier gap@8000 = +19.3 vs +21.8 @4000 (89% retained).
- B2 DID improve with budget (.747 -> .795 all-tier), so the arm discriminates:
  B2 was not step-saturated, it used the extra compute, and still lost ground
  on the hard tiers where V2 sits at ~.98.
- flops/correct at 8000: V2 7.8e9 vs B2 (computed from rows) — V2 still cheaper.

## Verdict

**BUDGET-ROBUST (gate 2 PASS, decisively).** The delta advantage is not a
convergence-speed artifact: doubling the training budget lets B2 improve yet
the tier-3-5 gap remains +25.8 pts. The "better architecture at this scale"
claim survives its strongest cheap attack. 50M scale check is GO (subject to
the throughput feasibility probe).
